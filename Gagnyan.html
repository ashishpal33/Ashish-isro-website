Gaganyan Mission

The Gaganyaan mission is India's human spaceflight program, aiming to send three-person crew to Low Earth Orbit (LEO) for a three-day mission and safely return them to Earth. The first crewed mission is planned for early 2027, preceded by uncrewed test flights. India will become the fourth nation to launch its own crewed spacecraft if successful.

Mission Objectives:

Demonstrate Human Spaceflight Capability :- The primary goal is to demonstrate India's ability to send humans into space and safely bring them back.

Build a Foundation for Human Space Exploration :- Gaganyaan will lay the groundwork for future Indian human space exploration programs, including space stations, lunar landings, and interplanetary missions.

Advance Science and Technology :- The mission will push the boundaries of Indian science and technology, improving capabilities in various fields.

Inspire and Motivate :- Gaganyaan aims to inspire the youth and encourage participation in science and technology.

Key Features:

Crew Module (CM) :- A truncated cone-shaped module designed to carry up to three astronauts.

Service Module (SM) :- Attached to the CM, it contains the propulsion and power systems.

Human Rated Launch Vehicle (HRLV) :- A modified version of the LVM3 rocket, India's most powerful rocket, designed for crewed spaceflight.

Uncrewed Test Flights :- Before the crewed mission, two uncrewed test flights and one with a humanoid robot (Vyommitra) are planned in 2025.

Orbit :- The crew will orbit Earth at an altitude of 400 kilometers for three days. Landing: The crew will land in Indian sea waters.

Timeline:

2025: Three uncrewed test flights, including one with a humanoid robot.

Early 2027: The first crewed Gaganyaan mission is planned.

Impact:

Enhanced Space Exploration Capabilities: Gaganyaan will significantly enhance India's space exploration capabilities and position it among the elite nations in human spaceflight.
