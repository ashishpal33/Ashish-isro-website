Overview of ISRO Satellite Programs

Communication Satellites (INSAT / GSAT series)

Serve telecommunication, TV broadcasting, disaster warning, maritime and strategic applications.Notable recent launches include INSAT‑3DS (Feb 2024) for climate and weather,and GSAT-N2 (Nov 2024) for enhanced comms.

Earth-Observation Satellites

Cartosat‑3 (Nov 2019): Panchromatic resolution of 0.25 m—the highest in its.Oceansat‑3 (EOS‑06) (Nov 2022): Equipped with scatterometer and Ocean Colour Monitor Other satellites: EOS‑04, EOS‑07, EOS‑08, etc., launched 2021–2025 for diverse climate and agricultural missions.

Navigation Satellites (NavIC/IRNSS)

The regional navigation system IRNSS comprises 9 satellites (8 operational), delivering geosynchronous navigation capabilities.GSAT‑7 and GSAT‑7A also support military communications and strategic mapping.

Scientific & Planetary Missions

AstroSat (2015): India’s first dedicated multi-wavelength observatory.Aditya‑L1 (Sep 2023): First solar observatory at L1, with instruments like SUIT for UV imaging Lunar missions: Chandrayaan‑1 (2008), Chandrayaan‑2 (2019 orbiter), Chandrayaan‑3 (landed 2023). Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) (2013): Successfully entered Martian orbit.

Meteorological Satellites

Kalpana‑1 (2002): First dedicated weather satellite, based on PSLV-C4

Experimental / Small & Student Satellites

Includes SRE‑1, TechSat, NanoSat, SPADEX‑A/B (space docking demo Jan 2025), Microsat‑TD, NVS‑02 (Jan 2025), among others.
