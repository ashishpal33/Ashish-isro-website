International Collaborations & Diplomacy

Joint satellite missions

Chandrayaan‑1 discovered water molecules on the Moon via payloads from NASA and ESA. Megha‑Tropiques & SARAL with CNES monitor climate and oceanography. NISAR—a NASA‑ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar satellite—will map Earth’s land and ice every ~12 days, with freely available data for all. ESA’s Proba‑3 solar research satellite launched on PSLV‑XL.

Commercial Launch Services

Affordable launch services: ISRO has launched over 400 foreign satellites from countries like USA, UK, Singapore, Germany, Brazil, and Nepal.Singapore partnership: Third-largest customer, with 20 satellites launched; six satellites launched on PSLV-C56 alone. Startups & private sector: Through IN‑SPACe and NSIL, ISRO targets a $4‑5 billion satellite launch revenue by 2033 with private players like Skyroot and Agnikul spearheading development.

Technological Leadership & Innovations

Satellite docking (SpaDeX): In January 2025, ISRO became the fourth nation to dock satellites in orbit—a major milestone for in-space servicing and modular spacecraft. Human spaceflight & space station: Training partnerships are underway with NASA, JAXA, and ESA; planned to launch Bharatiya Antariksh Station by 2035 with Gaganyaan as a precurso. Cryogenic & small launcher advances: Indigenous cryogenic engines, growing SSLV capabilities, and RLV development push India’s capacity to launch heavier payloads.

Earth Observation, Defense & Humanitarian Application

Environmental & agriculture monitoring: Satellites like IRS, Cartosat, Risat, and Megha-Tropiques support crop forecasting, cyclone tracking, oceanography, and climate-change data. Defense utility: Ten satellites—such as Cartosat, RISAT, GSAT-7/7A—provide 24/7 surveillance, nav-precision, and intel support for national security. Disaster response & soft power: Member of the International Charter on Space & Major Disasters; satellites aid regional disaster relief, boosting India’s global goodwill.

Socio-economic & Technology Transfer Impact.

Tech spin-offs: Adaptations such as low-cost artificial heart pumps, microprocessor-controlled prosthetic knees, LiDAR sensors, and urban crowd management tools extend ISRO’s tech into diverse sectors. Commercial tech transfers: Over 166 space tech innovations licensed to Indian industry, enhancing domestic auto, telecom, healthcare, and construction sectors. Education & training outreach: ISRO’s YUVIKA programme, UNNATI training, and Dehradun space-centre workshops nurture global talent and foster regional space proficiency.
